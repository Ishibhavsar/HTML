Headings in HTML

Understanding Headings

Headings are used to define the structure of your content. They range from <h1> to <h6>, with <h1> being the most important and <h6> the least.

Importance of Headings

Using headings correctly helps with SEO and makes your content more accessible. It allows readers to quickly scan and understand the main topics of your page.

Best Practices

  • Use only one <h1> per page.
  • Use headings in a logical order.
  • Avoid skipping heading levels (e.g., going from <h1> to <h3>).
  • Make headings descriptive and concise.
Example of Headings

Here is an example of how headings can be structured:

<h1>Main Title</h1>
<h2>Subsection Title</h2>
<h3>Sub-subsection Title</h3>
<h4>Further Details</h4>
<h5>Additional Information</h5>
<h6>Minor Details</h6>
Conclusion

Proper use of headings enhances readability and organization of your content, making it easier for users to navigate and understand.
